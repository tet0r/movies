Plot
Ken Park (2002) chronicles California skateboarders and their problems. Some seem relatively normal, such as Shawn (James Bullard), while others, like Tate (James Ransone) are dangerously full of rage. Several are burdened with strange families lives; Peaches (Tiffany Limos) has to take care of her father, a religious zealot, and wishes to run away, but can't bring herself to do it. The teenagers claim to be one another's support, and to be a tight-knit group, but are yet strangely unaware of what each is going through. In the end, the film is a portrait of alienation and what might be going on in everyday suburbia.
