Plot
Brian Piccolo (James Caan) and Gale Sayers (Billy Dee Williams) both started out in the 1960s playing for the Chicago Bears, Sayers in the draft and Piccolo as a walk-on. The rivalry starts when both men try out for the same position on the team. The coach, hoping to silence the rivalry, assigns Piccolo and Sayers as roommates while at training camp and on the road. In the racially-tense ‘60s, this decision to pair a black and a white caused a great deal of controversy, but in the end it paid off and the men became best friends. When Gale Sayers sustained a serious knee injury, it was his friend, Brian Piccolo, who helped give him the determination to see through the extensive rehabilitation and return to the team. As Sayer’s star rises, Piccolo starts feeling the effects of ill health but continues to push himself to play. Finally he breaks down and, after seeing a doctor, is found to have terminal cancer. During the period of time when Piccolo health is declining, Sayers and his wife stand staunchly behind him and his wife. They help encourage him after having surgery to remove the large tumor in his chest.
